Teaching Philosophy

Dr. Denison's pedagogical approach centers on the integration of traditional humanistic inquiry with innovative digital methodologies. They believe that technology should amplify rather than replace the core values of humanities education: critical thinking, ethical reflection, and cultural understanding.

In their courses, students engage with both timeless philosophical questions and contemporary technological challenges. Through hands-on projects, collaborative research, and reflective writing, students develop both technical skills and critical perspectives on the role of technology in society.

Core Principles

  • Active Learning: Students learn by doing, creating, and collaborating
  • Critical Engagement: Technology is approached critically, not uncritically adopted
  • Inclusive Pedagogy: Digital tools expand access and accommodate diverse learning styles
  • Public Scholarship: Student work contributes to broader public understanding
